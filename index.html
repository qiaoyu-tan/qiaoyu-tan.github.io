Biography

Dr. Qiaoyu Tan is an Assistant Professor in the Computer Science Department at NYU Shanghai, jointly affiliated with the Tandon School of Engineering at New York University. Before joining NYU Shanghai, he earned his Ph.D. from Texas A&M University under the supervision of Prof. Xia Hu, and received his B.Eng. from Southwest University, advised by Prof. Guoxian Yu. His research focuses on LLMs, autonomous agents, multimodal intelligence, graph learning, knowledge graphs, and recommendation systems. He has published over 35 peer-reviewed papers in top-tier venues such as ICML, NeurIPS, KDD, WWW, ACL, EMNLP, WSDM, CVPR, and IJCAI, accumulating over 2,000 citations. He received the Best Student Paper Finalist at AMIA 2023. He has successfully led several research projects as Principal Investigator and actively contributes to the academic community, serving as the Ph.D. Symposium Chair for CIKM 2025 and Program Chair for ICHI 2023. Before his current position, he worked as a research intern at Alibaba Damo Academy and Samsung Research America.

Research Interest

My research aims to develop accurate, universal, efficient, and trustworthy AI systems by bridging theoretical innovation with practical impact. Our team focuses on advancing the frontiers of large language models, multimodal intelligence, structured reasoning, and agentic search, with real-world applications spanning question answering, information retrieval, image generation, recommendation systems, bioinformatics, and healthcare.

  • Large Language Models: Multimodal Understanding and Generation, Retrieval-Augmented Generation, Post-Training Optimization, Agentic Reasoning, and Autonomous Agents.

  • Trustworthy Machine Learning: AI Safety, Adversarial Attacks, Defense Mechanisms, Responsible, and Interpretable AI Systems.

  • Graph Machine Learning: Graph Foundation Models, Graph RAG, Graph Learning, Graph Neural Networks, Graph Pre-Training, Graph Recommendation Systems.
